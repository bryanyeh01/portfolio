What This Project Is

Designed and validated the front brake rotor for a Formula SAE car as part of Highlander Racing.

The goal was to ensure the rotor could withstand realistic braking conditions by evaluating stress, deformation, and safety under combined thermal and mechanical loading. This included modeling braking torque from system-level calculations and applying experimentally derived temperature data.

Full Engine Assembly CAD

What I Worked On

  • Modeled the brake rotor geometry in SolidWorks
  • Calculated braking torque (374.53 N·m) from pedal force, brake bias, and system parameters
  • Set up transient thermal and structural simulations in ANSYS Workbench
  • Applied pad-contact heating and convection boundary conditions
  • Coupled thermal results into structural analysis to evaluate stress and deformation
  • Identified stress concentrations at rivet mounting regions
  • Evaluated realistic stress values by accounting for boundary condition artifacts
  • Determined factor of safety under both moderate (200°C) and severe (500°C) conditions
Engineering drawing I created for one of several injector plates

Where I’m At

The rotor design has been validated through transient thermal-structural simulation.

Results showed that the highest stresses occur at the mounting regions where braking torque is transferred, with a representative factor of safety around 2.6. The transient approach improved realism by separating heating and loading, avoiding the unrealistic stress spikes seen in earlier static models.

The design is now supported by simulation results and ready for further refinement or manufacturing consideration.
