What This Project Is

This project is centered on designing a pintle injector assembly that is not just conceptually correct, but practical to manufacture and hand off. The goal is to build something that survives real engineering review, with dimensions, interfaces, and drawing details clear enough that another person can actually make it without guessing.

A big part of the work has been balancing performance-oriented design ideas with manufacturability, packaging, and revision discipline. Instead of treating the CAD model as the final result, I’ve been treating it as part of a larger engineering package.

Design Priorities

Manufacturability first

I tried to keep the design realistic for common machining operations and avoid details that would make manufacturing harder than it needs to be.

Clear interfaces

Mating surfaces, hole patterns, and assembly relationships needed to be readable and well-defined so the part could be integrated without unnecessary ambiguity.

Clean documentation

The drawings are part of the deliverable, not an afterthought. The goal is for the design package to communicate intent clearly, not just look complete.

Iterative improvement

Instead of forcing one “final” version too early, I’ve been refining the design through revision and feedback so the project keeps moving toward a stronger result.

Where I’m At

Current State

The project is still in progress, but it has already reached a stage where the current geometry can be communicated clearly and evaluated physically. At this point, the work is less about coming up with the initial concept and more about tightening the design, improving details, and pushing it toward a more complete and build-ready state.
