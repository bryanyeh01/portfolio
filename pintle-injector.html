What This Project Is

Designed a pintle injector for a liquid rocket engine as part of Highlander Space Program’s Project Clementine.

Owned the injector from early geometry to final drawings, focusing on a design that is both functional and manufacturable. The injector supports an oxidizer-centered configuration and routes flow for both film cooling and regenerative cooling.

Full Engine Assembly CAD

What I Worked On

  • Designed injector geometry, including internal flow paths and interfaces
  • Sized flow areas and O-ring grooves for sealing and performance
  • Integrated film cooling (~11% of total flow) into injector design
  • Moved CAD from Fusion 360 to Onshape for team collaboration
  • Created engineering drawings used for manufacturing
  • Iterated design based on team reviews and build constraints
Engineering drawing I created for one of several injector plates

Where I’m At

Current State

The injector design is complete and fully documented with engineering drawings.

The team is preparing for final ANSYS simulations to validate flow behavior and performance before moving into manufacturing. My role is shifting toward supporting validation and ensuring the design is ready to be built.
