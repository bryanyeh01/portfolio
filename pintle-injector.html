Project

Pintle Injector Design

Design and documentation of a liquid rocket pintle injector plate, including engineering drawings for manufacturing and iterative geometry updates based on build constraints.

SolidWorks Manufacturing Drawings DFM Propulsion Hardware

Overview

The goal was to create a manufacturable injector plate design that could survive review and be handed off cleanly to manufacturing. I focused on clarity of specs, feature intent, and iteration discipline (change one thing, check it, document it).

What I Owned

  • CAD modeling of injector plate geometry and relevant interfaces
  • Engineering drawings for manufacturing: dimensions, tolerances, and callouts
  • Design iteration based on machining constraints and team feedback

Design Decisions

Manufacturability first

Kept features realistic for common machining operations and avoided geometry that would require exotic tooling.

Clear interfaces

Prioritized mating surfaces, bolt patterns, and stack-up clarity so assembly errors are less likely.

Documentation discipline

Every revision had a reason, and drawings were kept consistent so the shop can build without guessing.

Review-ready layout

Organized features and views so a reviewer can understand the design in under a minute.

Results / Deliverables

  • Injector plate CAD model ready for manufacturing handoff
  • Engineering drawings with critical dimensions + notes
  • Revision updates based on constraints discovered during review
