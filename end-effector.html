Project

Robotic End Effector

Mechanical design and rapid prototyping of an end-effector for automated fruit picking, focused on reliable grasping, suction integration, and iteration based on real test outcomes.

CAD Rapid Prototyping Mechanism Design Testing & Iteration

Overview

This project was about making a mechanism that works in real conditions. I designed the end-effector geometry, built prototypes, tested failure modes, and iterated the design until the grasp and release were consistent.

What I Owned

  • CAD design of the end-effector geometry and interfaces
  • Prototype build iterations (print → test → revise)
  • Design updates based on failure modes observed during testing

Key Design Goals

Reliable grasp

Geometry designed to help self-align and maintain contact under small misalignments.

Clean release

Built around consistent release behavior to reduce jams and missed picks.

Integration-ready

Kept mounting and interfaces clear so the end-effector can be swapped and tested quickly.

Iterate fast

Designed for quick prints and quick tests so improvements happened weekly, not monthly.

Results / Deliverables

  • End-effector CAD model + revision history
  • Prototype parts and test-backed design updates
  • Documented design decisions based on test outcomes
